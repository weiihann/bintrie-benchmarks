Binary Trie Benchmarks

Performance research for Ethereum's binary trie (EIP-7864)

Experiments

Group Depth Benchmarks
Compared all eight group-depth configurations (GD-1 through GD-8) on 360 GB databases (~400M state entries). Five benchmark types under verified cold-cache protocol: synthetic SLOAD/SSTORE and ERC20 contract workloads.
Result: The sweet spot is GD-5 or GD-6. GD-5 wins writes (+7% over GD-4), GD-6 leads reads (6.39 Mgas/s) and mixed workloads.
8 configurations 5 benchmark types ~700 runs
MPT vs Binary Trie
Head-to-head comparison of production MPT against optimized BT-GD5 on bare-metal AMD EPYC (48 cores, 126 GB RAM). ERC20 read and write workloads with 100 MPT runs and 10 BT runs under cold-cache protocol on ~1.5 TB databases.
Result: BT is ~1.7× slower on reads, ~2.5× on writes per storage slot. Raw Mgas/s (up to 9.6×) is misleading due to different EIP-4762 gas schedules.
2 architectures 3 benchmark types 330 runs
UBT vs PBT
Two binary trie variants at the same group depth: UBT (bitarray path encoding, single-pass commit) vs PBT (zone-partitioned key derivation across basic-data, code-chunk, storage-slot zones, with parallel per-zone commit). Three ERC20 benchmarks with 10 cold-cache runs each on ~50 GB databases (Intel Xeon 8358, 8 cores, 31 GB RAM).
Result: PBT wins every benchmark — +11.0% on reads, +20.6% on writes, +17.5% on mixed. Gains come from shorter per-zone paths and parallel hashing across zone subtries.
